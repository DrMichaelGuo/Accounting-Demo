Royal Society of Edinburgh Small Research Grant

The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on the Future of Accounting in the UK

An Investigation into Evolving Roles and Ethical Challenges

Principal Investigator

Dr. Michael Guo

University of the West of Scotland, UK

Research Summary

This research aims to examine the transformative impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) on the UK's accounting sector. Employing a mixed-methods approach, the research addresses a knowledge gap in empirical studies by investigating the first-hand experiences of accounting professionals navigating this technological shift.

Operational Efficiency

Investigating how AI technologies are enhancing productivity and streamlining accounting processes in UK firms.

Skill Set Evolution

Examining the changing competencies required for accounting professionals in an AI-augmented workplace.

Professional Adaptability

Analyzing how accounting professionals are adapting to technological changes and evolving their roles.

Ethical Challenges

Exploring issues such as data privacy, algorithmic fairness, and the potential for job displacement in the accounting sector.

The findings are positioned to offer valuable insights not only for the accounting industry but also for educational institutions, policymakers, and regulatory bodies, thereby making a significant contribution to the field.

Learn More

Research Methodology

This research adopts a mixed-methods approach, integrating both qualitative and quantitative data collection techniques. Qualitative data will be obtained through in-depth interviews with accounting professionals, while quantitative insights will be sourced from questionnaires distributed across the UK accounting sector.
